## Key Decisions

**Course Decisions:**
1. **Why Gumroad?** Simple setup, no course platform fees, own customer data
2. **Why video + code?** Developers learn by doing, need both explanation + code
3. **Why real patterns?** Too many AI courses are theoretical, mine is practical
4. **Why $129?** Premium pricing (signals quality), targets teams with budgets

**Content Decisions:**
1. **Focus on patterns** - Not "how to use ChatGPT" but "how to integrate AI into real apps"
2. **Show costs** - Every pattern includes $ analysis (developers care about budget)
3. **Battle-tested** - Only patterns I've used in production (3 client projects + FlowStack)
4. **Evergreen** - Patterns apply across AI models (not just GPT-4)

**Marketing Decisions:**
1. **Build in public** - Tweet progress weekly (build anticipation)
2. **Free samples on YouTube** - 3 lessons free to showcase quality
3. **Email list** - Collect emails with free "AI Patterns Cheatsheet" PDF
4. **Affiliate program** - 30% commission (other devs promote for me)

## Notes & Learnings

**What's Going Well:**
- Content quality high (beta testers love it)
- Real examples resonate (practical > theoretical)
- Code repo structure clean (easy to follow)
- Filming easier than expected (2 takes max per lesson)

**What's Challenging:**
- Video editing slow (6 hours per 1 hour of content)
- AI agents module needs more research (haven't built this yet)
- Marketing difficult (small audience, feels awkward promoting myself)
- Launch date pressure (Dec 15 is tight, considering Jan 5)

**Patterns Discovered:**
- Script ‚Üí Film ‚Üí Edit in batches (context switching kills productivity)
- Morning filming best (fresh voice, clear thinking)
- Edit next day (fresh eyes catch mistakes)
- Beta tester feedback invaluable (caught 3 confusing explanations)

**Personal Insights:**
- Teaching forces deep understanding (explaining ‚â† knowing)
- Showing real code (even messy) more valuable than perfect examples
- Developers appreciate honesty about costs, limitations
- Building in public creates accountability (can't quit when people watching)
